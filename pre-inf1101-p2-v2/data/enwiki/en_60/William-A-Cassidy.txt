William A. Cassidy (3 January 1928http://www.tributes.com/obituary/show/William-Cassidy-108333862 - 22 March 2020) was an American geologist and professor emeritus of Geology and Planetary Science at the University of Pittsburgh. Cassidy was responsible for recognizing that Antarctica represented the greatest repository of meteorites on earth.http://www.slate.com/articles/technology/future_tense/2013/09/the_best_meteorites_are_found_in_antarctica.html Starting in the early 1970s, Cassidy led most major meteorite expeditions to the south polar region, and in 1979, he was awarded the Antarctica Service Medal. Bill Cassidy founded and led ANSMET from 1976 to 1993. The Cassidy Glacier in Antarctica is named for him, the mineral Cassidyite, and the asteroid 3382 Cassidy. Cassidy assisted the NTSB with the investigation into TWA flight 800.
