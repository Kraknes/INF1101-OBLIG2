Pavlos Matesis (12 January 1933 - 20 January 2013) was a Greek novelist, playwright and translator.Profile He was born in Divri, a village in the Peloponnese and had a peripatetic youth. He studied acting, music and languages, and taught drama at the Stavrakou School in Athens (1963-64). He also worked as a writer at the National Theatre during 1971-73. He wrote scripts for two television series broadcast on the state channel (1974-76). His debut play The Ceremony was staged in 1967 and revived at the National Theatre in 1969. He wrote more than a dozen plays, most of which were performed at the National Theatre. He won several awards for his plays and for his fiction. As a translator, he translated the works of Ben Jonson, William Shakespeare, Harold Pinter, Fernando Arrabal, Antonin Artaud, Beaumarchais and William Faulkner. His own work has been translated into numerous European languages.
