Raziel Abelson (24 June 1921 - 14 June 2017) was an American academic. He served as Professor of Philosophy Emeritus at New York University and was a proponent of the Ordinary Language School of Philosophy.Library of Congress Authority File ==Biography== He was born in Brooklyn, New York, the son of Rabbi Alter Abelson and Anna Goldina Schwartz. His brother was the playwright Lionel Abel (1910-2001). He received a M.A. from the University of Chicago in 1950 with the thesis "Bertrand Russell's theory of truth",WorldCat item entry and a Ph.D. in 1960 from New York University with a thesis "An analysis of the concept of definition, and critique of three traditional philosophical views concerning its role in knowledge".WorldCat item entry Abelson died in June 2017 at the age of 95.RAZIEL ABELSON Obituary ==Works== * Abelson, Raziel Ethics and Metaethics NY: St Martin's, 1963 * Abelson, Raziel.
