Wheelerigobius is a genus of gobies native to the eastern Atlantic Ocean. The name of this genus honours the British ichthyologist Alwyne C. Wheeler (1929-2005) who was the curator of Fishes at the British Museum (Natural History). ==Species== There are currently two recognized species in this genus: * Wheelerigobius maltzani (Steindachner, 1881) * Wheelerigobius wirtzi P. J. Miller, 1988 (Cameroon goby) ==References== Category:Gobiidae
