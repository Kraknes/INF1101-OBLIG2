The Nur ul-Ihsan Mosque was, until 2018, the oldest mosque in Phnom Penh, the capital of Cambodia. It was situated 7 km north of the centre of the city. It was built in 1813https://cityseeker.com/phnom-penh/297154-nur-ul-ihsan-mosque by the Cham community. It survived the Khmer rouge regime which transformed it into a pigsty.http://travel.yahoo.com/p-travelguide-2760768-nur_ul_ihsan_mosque_phnom_penh-i In 2018 it has been destroyed and replaced by a mosque called the KM7 Mosque, a Middle Eastern design financed by a donation from Kuwait. ==References== Mosque KM7 remplacing the historical mosque.
