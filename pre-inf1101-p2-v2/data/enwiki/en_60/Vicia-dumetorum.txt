Vicia dumetorum is a species of legume in the vetch genus. ==Description== Vicia dumetorum can reach a height of . This herbaceous perennial, erect plant is quite rare and variable. Leaves are pinnate, with 3-5 pairs of ovate leaflets, long. It produces stalked clusters with 3-12 bluish-violet flowers. They bloom from May to June. ==Distribution== This species is present in Central, Northern and Eastern Europe and in the Western Asia. ==Habitat== Vicia dumetorum can be found in calcareous mountainous woodland, in deciduous, open woodlands and in hedgerows at elevation of above sea level.
