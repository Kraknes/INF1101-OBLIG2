Reignwood Centre in Jianwai Subdistrict, Chaoyang District, Beijing Reignwood Group () is an independent Chinese investment company owning brands and growth companies in China, the rest of Asia, the UK and the US. It was founded in 1984 by Dr Chanchai Ruayrungruang, a Thai-Chinese entrepreneur with strong roots in China. Reignwood's investments span a range of sectors giving it exposure across a multiple number of growth industries in different countries. The primary sectors of activity include consumer products, hotels and residential property, golf courses, wellness centres, travel agencies, aviation, offshore engineering and financing. Countries that Reignwood is active in include China, Singapore, the US and the UK. Some of the most significant consumer products include the distribution of Red Bull and other drinks in China. In July 2014, Reignwood purchased a 25% stake in All Market, producer of Vita Coco and in 2016 acquired a majority control of Voss. The most notable brands in the UK are Ten Trinity Square - a mixed-use scheme including the Four Seasons Hotel London, Residences and Ten Trinity Square Private Club https://www.ft.com/content/c8cce5ac-e7a8-11e6-967b-c88452263daf}} and Wentworth Club. Originally designed by renowned architect Sir Edwin Cooper, it was built in 1922 as the headquarters of the Port of London Authority. On 19th July 2019, the development was announced as the winner of the annual City Heritage Award.
