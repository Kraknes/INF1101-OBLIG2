Sheung Wo HangAntiquities and Monuments Office: Leaflet about the Kang Yung Study Hall is a Hakka village in Sha Tau Kok, in the North District of Hong Kong. It is occupied by members of Hakka Li (Li ) Clan. Until the 18th century, the village was known as Wo Hang (). ==History== Before the arrival of the Lis, Wo Hang was occupied by the Hos (), the Tsangs () and the Tangs (). Lee Tak-wah (), a member of the Lis, moved to Wo Hang in 1698, shortly after the end of the Great Clearance. Kuen-lam (), son of Tak-wah, built an Ancestral Hall in the village. The feng shui of the hall was considered harmful to the Hos, the Tsangs and the Tangs and they left the village.Historic Building Appraisal: Lee Sze Sai Kui, Entrance Gate, Nos. 86-88 Sheung Wo Hang Members of the Li Clan later branched out to found the two nearby villages of Ha Wo Hang ("lower Wo Hang") and Wo Hang Tai Long (), in around 1730 and 1750 respectively. Wo Hang was subsequently renamed Sheung Wo Hang.Historic Building Appraisal: Wing Kat Tong, No. 18A Sheung Wo Hang Since the 1950s many inhabitants of the village have moved to urban areas in Hong Kong and abroad. ==Built heritage== The Kang Yung Study Hall () was built in the early Qing Dynasty by the Li Clan. The study hall was later converted into a public school, which closed in 1986. It is now a declared monument. Other historical buildings include: * Entrance Gate of the Lee Sze Sai Kui (), the Li ancestral hall. Built in the 18th century.
