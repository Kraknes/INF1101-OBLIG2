The Ralfes Creek, a perennial stream that is part of the Hastings River catchment, is located in the Mid North Coast region of New South Wales, Australia. ==Course and features== Ralfes Creek rises within the Great Dividing Range, about northeast of the Oxley Trigonometrical Station, within the Doyles River State Forest. The river flows generally southeast, northeast, southeast, and then north northeast before reaching its confluence with the Hastings River west of the locality of Ellenborough. The river descends over its course.
