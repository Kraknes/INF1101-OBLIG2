Manitoba was a system-on-a-chip (SoC) introduced by Intel Corporation in 2003. It was a mostly unsuccessful attempt by Intel to break into the smart phones market. The chip integrated flash memory, a digital signal processor and an XScale processor core.Manitoba, Intel's smart-phone "wireless Internet on a chip" is here , Matthew Broersma, CNet Asia, February 13, 2003.
