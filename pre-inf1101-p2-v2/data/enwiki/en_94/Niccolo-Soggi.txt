Baldo Magini with a model of the Church of San Fabiano, painting by Niccolo Soggi (1522), in the Prato Cathedral. Niccolo Soggi (c. 1480 - 12 July 1552) was an Italian painter, born in Monte San Savino in the Province of Arezzo, Italy. He was a pupil of Pietro Perugino, and was in Rome during the pontificate of Pope Leo X. Soggi then moved to Prato, where Baldo Magini was his principal patron. He eventually settled in Arezzo. Among his pupils was Papino della Pieve. == References == * Baldini, Nicoletta, Niccolo Soggi, Florence, Edifir, 1997. * Vasari, Giorgio, Le Vite delle piu eccellenti pittori, scultori, ed architettori, many editions and translations.
