Wind power in Finland describes wind power in Finland as part of energy in Finland and renewable energy in Finland. In 2018 the cumulative wind power capacity in Finland was 2,041 MW compared to Sweden 7,047 MW, Ireland 3,564 MW and Germany 59,311 MW. In 2018 there was zero new installed wind power in Finland. Tuulivoimayhdistys == Employment == According to EPV Tuulivoima in 2010, a 100 MW wind farm (30 wind turbines) would employ ca 1000 persons.Tuulivoiman rakentaminen tyollistaisi tuhansia YLE 18.3.2010 == In practice == In 2016, St1 opened the 59,5 MW TuuliWatti at a cost of EUR140 million. Wind power is partially responsible for keeping electricity prices from rising. According to Technical University of Lappeenranta wind power became the cheapest power in Finland since March 2017. Power production cost per MWh in 2017 were: wind power 41 EUR, nuclear power 42 EUR, peat 61 EUR, coal 64EUR, wood 76EUR and solar 100EUR. In 2019 new wind power cost is estimated to be 25 EUR/MWh.
