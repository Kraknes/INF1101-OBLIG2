Andersen Gabrych is an American comic book writer and actor. ==Biography== Gabrych grew up in Chico, California,Out Jun 2006 where he went by the name of Andy. He was active in drama at Chico Senior High School and then graduated in 1991. As an actor, he has appeared in the films Edge of Seventeen, Boys Life 4: Four Play, Gypsy 83, Hit and Runway, and Another Gay Movie (and its sequel). Off-Broadway he has appeared in productions at The Public Theater, New York Theater Workshop, Ensemble Studio Theater, the Westbeth, and Here! He has directed "Tied Up in Knotts", a one-woman show, which is the story of growing up with her comedian father, for Karen Knotts, a stand-up comic and SAG/AFTRA actor. He has always been a fan of comic books, and bought his first comic was Justice League of America when he was aged 7 years old. As a writer he has written Batman (#634, #642), Detective Comics (#790-800, #808-810), Batgirl (#38, #58-73), Catwoman (#43), and Omega Men (#1-6). He is a regular contributor and travel writer for LA Confidential Magazine. In 2010, Gabrych's original graphic novel Fogtown was published by Vertigo Comics under the Vertigo Crime imprint, a collaboration with artist Brad Rader.
