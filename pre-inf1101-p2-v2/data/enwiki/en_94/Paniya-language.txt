Paniya is one of the South Dravidian languages spoken in India. It is spoken by the Paniya people, a scheduled tribe with a majority of its speakers in the state of Kerala. The language is also known as Pania, Paniyan and Panyah. It belongs to the Dravidian family of languages.Linguistic Lineage for Paniya According to the 1981 Census, there were 63,827 speakers of Paniya which includes 56,952 in Kerala, 6,393 in Tamil Nadu, 482 in Karnataka.Ethnologue report for language code:pcg Most of its speakers are found in the Wayanad, Kozhikode, Kannur and Malappuram districts of Kerala, and to the west of the Nilgiri Hills in Tamil Nadu. ==See also== * List of Indian languages by number of native speakers ==References== ==Other external links== * http://www.languageinindia.com/may2001/paniya2.html * https://web.archive.org/web/20110526072200/http://apha.confex.com/apha/135am/techprogram/paper_163341.htm Category:Malayalam language Category:Languages of Kerala
