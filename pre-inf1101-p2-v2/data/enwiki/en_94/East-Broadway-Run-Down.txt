East Broadway Run Down is a 1966 album by jazz saxophonist Sonny Rollins, his last album before industry pressures led him to take a six-year hiatus.Wynn, Ron. (October 19, 1998) Survivors of stature: Rollins, Rush roll on Weekly Wire. Accessed November 15, 2007. The album represents one of his more notable experiments with free jazz,, p.418 according to The New Grove Dictionary of Jazz illustrating "the furthest extent to which he incorporated noise elements into his playing".Sonny Rollins. From The New Grove Dictionary of Jazz. Hosted at PBS. Accessed November 15, 2007. It has been critically described as among his 60s "jewels". Initially released on Impulse!
