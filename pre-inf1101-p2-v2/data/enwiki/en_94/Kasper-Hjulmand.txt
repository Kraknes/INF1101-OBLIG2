Kasper Hjulmand (born 9 April 1972) is a Danish football manager and a former player. He is currently the head coach for the Danish national team. ==Career== Hjulmand began his career with Randers Freja in 1987 where he played four years, and then signed with Herlev IF in the winter of 1992. He played with Herlev IF two seasons and then moved to B.93 in the winter of 1995.Kasper Hjulmands Stats After three seasons with B93 at the age of 26 Hjulmand was forced to retire as a football player due to a knee injury.Tuchels Nachfolger ist da ==Coaching career== ===Early career=== Hjulmand became head coach of Lyngby from 1 January 2006 until 7 July 2008 when he became an assistant coach with FC Nordsjaelland. He was named the successor of Morten Wieghorst at FC Nordsjaelland taking over from 1 July 2011 until 18 May 2014 when he had his final match as head coach of Nordsjaelland; a 2-2 draw against Brondby. Nordsjaelland won their first Danish championship with Hjulmand as head coach. ===FSV Mainz 05=== On 15 May 2014, Hjulmand was confirmed as Thomas Tuchel's successor at 1. FSV Mainz 05 for the following season. His first two matches in charge was in the Third qualifying round of the Europa League. In the first leg against Asteras Tripoli, Mainz won 1-0, and in the second leg, Mainz lost 3-1 and they got knocked out of the campaign. Then Mainz were knocked out in the first round of the DFB Cup after losing to Chemnitzer FC in a shoot-out. In his first eight league matches, Mainz were undefeated and in third place. After this, Mainz had won only one of their 13 matches and dropped down to 14th place. At this point, on 17 February 2015, he was sacked. His final match was a 4-2 loss to Borussia Dortmund. ===Return to Nordsjaelland=== On 15 December 2015, it was announced that Hjulmand would return as manager of Nordsjaelland as of 1 January 2016.FC Nordsjaelland far nye ejere og skifter cheftraener, FC Nordsjaelland, 15 December 2015 Hjulmand managed to get the team back on track and the club finished 9th in the 2015-16 Danish Superliga. In the following two seasons he led Nordsjaelland to a 6th and 3rd place.
