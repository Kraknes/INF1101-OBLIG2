Alfred's blind skink (Dibamus alfredi) is a species of blind lizards in the family Dibamidae. The species is endemic to Southeast Asia. ==Related species== Another species of Dibamus once had been referred to as Alfred's blind skink, but is now a separate species, Dibamus dezwaani. ==Geographic range== Alfred's blind skink is found in Thailand, Malaysia, and Nias. ==Habitat== D. alfredi can be found in primary forests, scrub, secondary forests, and plantations, at altitudes up to . ==Naming== Also known as Alfred's flat lizard and Alfred's limbless skink, D. alfredi is named after ichthyologist Eric R.
