Carlo Sgombrino (1619 - October 1686) was a Roman Catholic prelate who served as Bishop of Catanzaro (1672-1686) (in Latin) (in Latin) and Bishop of Belcastro (1652-1672). (in Latin) (in Latin) ==Biography== Carlo Sgombrino was born in 1619 in Airola, Italy. On 11 December 1652, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Innocent X as Bishop of Belcastro. On 29 December 1652, he was consecrated bishop by Marcantonio Franciotti, Cardinal-Priest of Santa Maria della Pace, with Ranuccio Scotti Douglas, Bishop Emeritus of Borgo San Donnino, serving as co-consecrator. On 8 February 1672, he was appointed during the papacy of Pope Clement X as Bishop of Catanzaro.
