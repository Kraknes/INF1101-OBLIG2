Cognitive Neuropsychology is a peer-reviewed academic journal aimed at promoting the investigation of human cognition that is based on neuropsychological methods including brain pathology, recording, stimulation, brain imaging or the study of developmental deficits. The journal is published eight times a year by Taylor and Francis and its joint editors-in-chief are Brenda Rapp (Johns Hopkins University) and Bradford Z. Mahon (University of Rochester).
