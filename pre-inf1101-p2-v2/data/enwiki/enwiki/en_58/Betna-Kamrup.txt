Betna is a village in Kamrup rural district, in the state of Assam, India, situated in north bank of river Brahmaputra. This place used to be Capital of King Amritta. ==Transport== The village is located north of National Highway 27 and connected to nearby towns and cities like Baihata, Rangiya and Guwahati with regular buses and other modes of transportation. ==See also== * Baruajani * Bhalukghata ==References== Category:Villages in Kamrup district
